# Copywriting Guide

This guide keeps only independently worded principles derived from public reference analysis. It does not reproduce reference-video transcripts, book excerpts, reviews, or highlight text.

## Working Method

- Start with an immediately resonant sentence. Do not spend the opening explaining the book.
- Use short spoken lines. Let pauses and ordinary wording carry the emotion.
- Begin with a concrete moment, object, action, or memory before moving toward an idea.
- Use the book and author as credibility anchors. Do not turn the script into a synopsis, review essay, or lecture.
- Let the emotional movement go from a specific life moment to an experience the audience can recognize.
- Keep the visual atmosphere independent from the exact wording. The picture should create feeling first.
- End on a sentence with emotional aftertaste. Do not add a CTA or return to sales language.

## Voice

The preferred voice is plainspoken, restrained, and close to lived experience. It may be sad, clear-eyed, warm, or firm, but it should not sound superior to the viewer.

Avoid:

- “你是不是” marketing openings;
- “不是……而是……” as a repeated AI-style argument pattern;
- long chains of parallel slogans;
- academic explanation of themes, characters, or author history;
- forced summaries such as “这本书告诉我们”;
- direct selling, cart references, or CTA language.

## Review Checklist

Before sending a script for approval, check:

1. Does the first line create an immediate emotional stop?
2. Are most lines short enough to speak naturally?
3. Is there at least one concrete scene or action?
4. Does the book enter naturally without taking over the opening?
5. Does the ending leave a feeling rather than a conclusion or sales pitch?
6. Is the wording original rather than copied from a source passage or review?
